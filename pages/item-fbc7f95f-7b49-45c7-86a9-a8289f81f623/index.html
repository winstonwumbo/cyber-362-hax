Wireshark Interface

Wireshark’s main interface is divided into three primary panes:

  1. Packet List Pane (Top Pane): Displays a summary of each captured packet — including packet number, timestamp, source, destination, protocol, length, and info. Each line represents one packet. When you click on a packet here, the other panes show details about it. 
  2. Packet Details Pane (Middle Pane or Left Pane): Shows the decoded, hierarchical breakdown of the selected packet. You can expand protocol layers (Ethernet, IP, TCP, HTTP, etc.) to see specific fields and values. 
  3. Packet Bytes Pane (Bottom Pane or Right Pane): Displays the raw data of the selected packet in hexadecimal and ASCII format. Highlighting a field in the middle pane also highlights the corresponding bytes here.
